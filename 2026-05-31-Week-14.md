## 💡 Key Concepts

### PC Troubleshooting and Diagnostics

Module 4 focused heavily on troubleshooting hardware and operating system issues.

Topics included:

* BIOS and UEFI
* Secure Boot
* Trusted Platform Module (TPM)
* POST and beep codes
* Boot failures and recovery
* MBR and GPT partitioning
* RAID troubleshooting
* SMART diagnostics
* BSOD analysis
* Overheating and performance issues

One of the most valuable lessons was learning how important a structured troubleshooting process is. Rather than guessing, technicians need to gather information, isolate causes, test solutions, and verify results methodically.

### Storage Reliability and System Performance

I also learned about storage-related failures and performance bottlenecks.

Topics included:

* Drive health monitoring
* SMART diagnostics
* SSD and HDD reliability
* RAID recovery
* Thermal throttling
* Performance troubleshooting

This helped me understand how hardware failures often present themselves through symptoms long before complete failure occurs.

### Networking Fundamentals

Module 5 introduced a large amount of networking content.

Topics included:

* LAN, WAN, WLAN, MAN, PAN, and SAN
* SOHO and Enterprise networks
* Network Interface Cards (NICs)
* MAC addresses
* Switches and patch panels
* Power over Ethernet (PoE)
* Structured cabling

Networking feels like one of the most important foundations for both IT support and cybersecurity because almost every system relies on communication between devices.

### Wireless Technologies

The module also covered wireless communication and Wi-Fi standards.

Topics included:

* Wireless access points
* Wi-Fi frequency bands
* 802.11 standards
* Wi-Fi 5 and Wi-Fi 6
* Channel selection
* MIMO and MU-MIMO
* Bluetooth
* RFID
* NFC

I found it particularly interesting to learn how wireless technologies balance speed, range, interference, and device compatibility.
