# 💡 Key Concepts

## Motherboards and PC Components

This week covered how the motherboard acts as the central communication hub for the computer and how all major components connect together.

Topics included:

* CPU sockets
* RAM slots and DIMMs
* PCIe expansion slots
* SATA and M.2 storage interfaces
* Power connectors and motherboard headers

Understanding how everything physically connects together helped me better visualize how computers are built and upgraded.

---

## Modern and Legacy Connectors

I also studied a large variety of cable standards and interfaces, including:

* USB standards and speeds
* USB-C and Thunderbolt
* HDMI and DisplayPort
* SATA and eSATA
* VGA, DVI, and serial cables

One important takeaway was seeing how technology constantly evolves, with older standards slowly being replaced by faster and more versatile interfaces.

---

## Display Technologies

This module also introduced:

* IPS, TN, and VA display panels
* OLED and Mini-LED technology
* Refresh rates and resolutions
* Video bandwidth concepts

I found it interesting how display technology impacts gaming, productivity, media consumption, and professional work differently depending on the use case.
