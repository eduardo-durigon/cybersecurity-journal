# Weekly Cybersecurity Journal – Week 18

## Overview

This week marked the beginning of my CompTIA A+ Core 1 exam preparation phase.

After completing all ten learning modules, I shifted my focus from learning new concepts to reinforcing my understanding through practice exams and hands-on labs. My primary goal this week was to identify knowledge gaps and become familiar with the style and difficulty of the official CompTIA exam questions.

---

## Topics Studied

### CompTIA A+ Core 1 Practice Assessments

This week I focused on completing the practice assessments available within the official CompTIA learning platform.

Activities included:

* Module practice quizzes
* Official CompTIA practice questions
* Performance-based questions (PBQs)
* Reviewing incorrect answers
* Identifying weaker knowledge areas for further study

One of the biggest takeaways was realizing that the official practice questions are significantly more challenging than the end-of-module quizzes. Much like my experience while preparing for the Tech+ certification, the practice exams were a humbling reminder that understanding a concept and applying it under exam conditions are two very different things.

---

### Hands-On Labs

Alongside the practice assessments, I completed the labs provided in the CompTIA learning material.

These labs reinforced topics covered throughout Core 1, including:

* Hardware installation and configuration
* Mobile device support
* Network configuration
* Peripheral setup
* Troubleshooting procedures
* Practical IT support scenarios

Working through the labs helped connect the theoretical knowledge from the modules with the practical skills expected in real-world IT support.

---

## Key Takeaways

This week reminded me that certification preparation isn't just about finishing the course—it's about proving to yourself that you can apply what you've learned.

The official CompTIA practice questions exposed areas where I was less confident and challenged me to think more critically about troubleshooting and problem-solving. While seeing lower scores than expected can be discouraging at first, it's exactly what practice exams are meant to reveal before the real exam.

The hands-on labs were equally valuable, reinforcing the importance of practical experience alongside theoretical knowledge. Every lab completed increased my confidence in performing common IT support tasks rather than simply recognizing the correct answer on a multiple-choice question.

Although the week was challenging, it gave me a much clearer understanding of where I stand and what I need to improve before sitting for the certification exam.
