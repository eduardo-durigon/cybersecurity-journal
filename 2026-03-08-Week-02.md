2️⃣ Key Concepts Learned

Personally Identifiable Information (PII):
PII refers to any data that can identify an individual directly or indirectly. Direct PII includes items like names, passport numbers, and bank information. Indirect PII includes demographic or descriptive data that can identify someone when combined with other information.

Encryption Fundamentals:
Encryption transforms readable information (plaintext) into unreadable data (ciphertext). Modern encryption relies on complex mathematical algorithms and encryption keys, often 128-bit or stronger, to protect data from unauthorized access.

Data at Rest vs Data in Transit:
Data at rest refers to stored data such as files on hard drives or databases. Data in transit refers to data moving across networks. Both require protection through encryption and secure protocols.

Password Security:
Strong password practices include avoiding reused passwords, avoiding dictionary words, and preventing predictable patterns. Password managers can help generate and securely store strong credentials.
